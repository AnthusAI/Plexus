# Agent Misrepresentation Guidelines

## Purpose
This score evaluates whether agents make false or misleading statements during SelectRx enrollment calls. Agents who follow standard scripting and provide accurate information receive a **"Yes"** (compliant). Agents who make misrepresentations receive a **"No"** (violation).

## Core Principle
Agents must accurately explain that SelectRx is a **service** with no enrollment or service fees, while clearly communicating that customers remain responsible for medication copays as determined by their insurance.

## Violation Categories

### 1. Copay Guarantees
**VIOLATION:** Any statement guaranteeing copay amounts will stay the same, decrease, or match current costs.

**Examples of Violations:**
- "Your copays stay the same"
- "Copays don't change" 
- "If you're paying nothing now, you'll pay nothing with SelectRx"
- "If you don't have copays, you won't have any here"
- "Whatever you're paying now, that stays the same"
- "Your copays should get the same, if not similar"

**Acceptable Language:**
- "Your copays will continue to be determined by your insurance carrier"
- "Copays are based on your insurance plan"
- "You're responsible for your prescription copays"

### 2. Free Medication Claims
**VIOLATION:** Stating medications themselves are free to the patient.

**Examples of Violations:**
- "Medications will be free"
- "No cost for your medications"
- "You won't pay anything for medications"

**Acceptable Language:**
- "Free delivery of your medications" (delivery is free, not the medications)
- "No delivery fees"
- "Free shipping"

### 3. Ambiguous Cost Language
**VIOLATION:** Using "free" or "no cost" language without clarifying that medication copays still apply.

**Key Rule:** Agents CAN say SelectRx enrollment/service is "at no cost" or "no additional cost" AS LONG AS they don't imply medications are also free. Clarification about copay responsibility is recommended but not required to be immediate.

**Acceptable Language:**
- "The SelectRx service is at no additional cost. Your copays will continue to be determined by your insurance carrier"
- "There's no cost for the service. The only responsibility you have is if you have a prescription copay"
- "The packaging, shipping and delivery is available to you at no additional cost"

### 4. Pharmacy Confusion
**VIOLATION:** Misleading statements about the pharmacy transition that suggest the customer can use both pharmacies interchangeably for the same medications.

**Examples of Violations:**
- "CVS will still be your pharmacy" (without proper qualification)
- "You can use both pharmacies for your regular medications"
- Suggesting customers can choose month-to-month which pharmacy to use

**Acceptable Language:**
- "Your current pharmacy will no longer be [prior pharmacy] and you will get your medications through SelectRx"
- "You can still use your local pharmacy for emergencies, controlled substances, or as-needed antibiotics"
- "SelectRx will be your primary pharmacy"

**Important:** Agents should NOT say "[pharmacy] will still be your pharmacy" even with qualifiers, as customers may choose to keep some medications at their local pharmacy by preference, not just for emergencies.

### 5. False Service Claims
**VIOLATION:** Factually incorrect statements about SelectRx capabilities or coverage.

**Examples:**
- Incorrect statements about controlled substances
- False claims about formulary coverage
- Guaranteeing specific medication availability without verification

### 6. Unqualified Savings Promises
**VIOLATION:** Promising cost savings without appropriate insurance disclaimers.

**Examples of Violations:**
- "Will definitely save you money"
- "Guaranteed to lower your costs"
- "Will bring down your copays"

## Copay Assistance Policy

**CRITICAL:** SelectRx does NOT have specialists dedicated to helping customers lower their copays or providing financing assistance.

**Violations:**
- "We can help you with your copays"
- "We'll work with you on copays"
- "We have specialists who can help with copay costs"
- "We can explore options to reduce your copays"

**Correct Response to Copay Questions:**
- Defer copay questions to the insurance carrier
- For general payment assistance (groceries, rent), agents can provide a reference link but SelectRx does not help directly

## Key Distinctions

### Service vs. Medication Costs
- **Service costs** (enrollment, packaging, delivery) = can be described as free
- **Medication costs** (copays) = always determined by insurance, never free unless insurance specifies

### Conditional Language
- Conditional "if...then" statements about copays are still violations when they guarantee amounts
- "If you have no copays now, you'll have none with us" = VIOLATION
- Explaining how insurance works generally = ACCEPTABLE

## Standard Acceptable Language
- "No additional cost for the service"
- "You pay your copays as determined by your insurance"
- "Free shipping and delivery"
- "Copays are determined by your insurance plan"
- "The service includes packaging, sorting, and delivery at no additional cost"

## Evaluation Note
When evaluating calls, focus on whether the agent made explicit false guarantees or misleading claims. Standard scripting that accurately describes the service and insurance responsibility should be marked as compliant ("Yes"). Only clear misrepresentations should be marked as violations ("No").
